About Me

I completed my undergraduate with a degree in computer science and a minor in mathematics at Georgia Southern University. I have always had a love for technology and the further I got in school the more I realized that I enjoyed writing about the technology that I was learning about. In the projects I was doing for my classes, I jumped to work on the different types of specification documents that would explain how the code would produce a type of interface or the reasoning behind wanting to do a specific task. My ability to do this gave me the opportunity to receive a job offer and move to Virginia.

During my undergraduate studies, I did a large amount of research and papers in relation to computer security, data communications and networking, software development, and many other technological studies. The complex topics of both computer science and mathematics pushed me to accomplish opportunities of learning how to research, communicate, form ideas, and write those propositions on paper. The highlight of my degree was to vocalize the ideas of intricate theories and objectives, and it was something that I thrived in. During this time in school, I knew I wanted to write, but I did not want to give up my love for technology. With a bit of research I was able to find that technical writing was the career choice for me.

I currently work as a technical writer for a contractor with Lockheed Martin Inc. in Manassas, Va. I started off as the sole technical writer for my program and was responsible for writing technical manuals that will be provided to the Department of Defense. The project I was first involved in supports the International Submarine Program Division which provides information on the hardware and software that the users will interact with on the ships. In completion of this program, I have completed fourteen manuals to the customer. The current project I have the opportunity to work with now involves the use of S1000D and XML to provide Interactive Electronic Technical Manuals (IETMs) to the customer.

When first starting this job, I was very new to writing and the complex information that comes with submarines. I began with doing a great deal of self training, research and speaking to the engineers around me on a daily basis. It was a challenging learning curve that I had to accomplish alone, but the work I provided has shown that I am more than capable of the difficulty of this career. I have come to love and appreciate the hard work that comes with the challenges of this kind of writing.
