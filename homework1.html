Homework One

Prompt

Review the digital text version of Chaucer’s Troilus and Criseyde located at this URL: http://www.librarius.com/troicris.htm In your first post, describe what makes this text unique from a traditional text. What considerations is it making for the audience? For learning online? How is the story organized to both construct a narrative and use the multimodal capabilities of the web? How did this hypertext’s design help you learn about Chaucer (e.g., what did you learn?) -OR- How did its design hinder your ability to read or enjoy the text? Try to find another example of a digital text version of a piece of literature (contemporary or classical) and compare its design to this version of Chaucer’s Troilus and Criseyde. In what ways does this other text’s design improve upon (or hinder) reader interaction and learning?

Response

Traditional text is very straight forward. There is a cover, table of contents, and then the text, but text that is found on the internet has many features that make it essential and more useful for learning. There are multiple unique things about text that are found online. In regards to the Libarious site, they have hyperlinks that take you directly to a chapter or page instead of flipping through a book, you scroll through the display instead of turning the page, and you can easily navigate to different areas of the book based on the reader's preference.

A major consideration that Liberious takes for assisting in the learning experience for users is the linked dictionary. The reader is able to click a link of difficult words and immediately see the definition for it, which is not something that is available or feasible for traditional text. Considerations were also taken when adding a dark and light mode, which allows for readers to decide how the text would be easier for them to read.

The Libarious site is very well organized in the sense that the books are separated into their own sections, the text is shown in the main display traditionally, and the glossary is placed at the bottom of the page with helpful visibility. Multimodal capabilities include several factors such as, visual layout, navigational features, content formatting, etc. Libarious has many features that organize the narrative in a positive way but the way that it is visually placed and formatted is not constructed in a way that is beneficial for the reader. The hyperlinks are extremely beneficial in learning when you are able to easily access a glossary at the click of the mouse, but when reading, the hyperlinks can be extremely distracting. There are so many on the page that it can cause the reader to lose focus on the story, and this is something that could be easily fixed by adding a hover feature into the CSS code when developing the website. This allows the text to be consistent until the user hovers over an unknown word that then it will show as a hyperlink to click on.

Kindle is a prime example of seamless reading that has many features including, highlighting, embedded glossary, links to other sections of the text, dark and light mode, and even allows the user to change the font and size of the text. This is a phenomenal way to enhance the reading experience for so many users. Amazon Kindle has a large selection of free contemporary and classical texts for the reader to learn and experience in a productive manner.

Traditional text is something that will always be available and accessible, but with the available technology that users have now text online truly enhances the reading experience to further their learning.
