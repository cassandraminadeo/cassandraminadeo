Homework Five

Prompt

Locate a website, app, game, or software interface that you think it particularly ugly, confusing, or hard to interact with. Provide the URL for the website or screen captures of the game/app and include a brief rhetorical analysis of its features focusing on audience perspective and use. As a user yourself, how did you respond to this decide? Choosing a poorly designed web site is based on your own opinion and experience but will be rooted in theories we’ve learned. Describe why you think this is a poor design using what we’ve learned about visual design. What small improvements could be made to improve the design?

Response

Goodreads is one of the best applications to track your books, compare with friends, and see reviews on the things you would like to read. It is so convenient to have a place that does all these things for a user, but the issue is that unlike comparing it to their app, their website is atrocious. It is difficult to navigate, things are proportioned incorrectly, and it is just overall, hard to use.

Starting off with just opening the website (once logged in) I am immediately lost with the amount of things that are happening.

There are advertisements, a Book Giveaway, my Currently Reading, more advertisements, News and Interviews, Reading Challenges, and Updates. As a user, I would not even know where to start. This is just too busy and so much going on that it could be overwhelming for the person on the site. The way everything is formatted is also very proportionally off. The advertisement at the top is large but doesn't cross the entire page. The content to the left of the feed does not match the size of the content to the right of the feed, and then the feed itself is not even fully centered on the screen, which makes everything feel so uneven and wonky.

At the very top of the screen, there are also two different versions of dropdowns.

This is just unnecessarily complicated for a first time or even an often user to navigate and understand where to go for anything. The navigation tab is overly complicated and would take a user an excessive amount of time to figure out where they need to go and find things. This also causes a huge issue of hidden features.

It is very confusing why the website was not given as much attention as the smartphone application did. On a smartphone everything is so organized and coordinated that it is way easier for the user to navigate.

I personally use Goodreads for all my reading, and I love the premise and what it is able to accomplish, but I would never consider opening up my browser and opening it there. I only use it on my phone, but it would be so great to see improvements on the website to reach more of an audience.

The website can be improved in so many ways. For example, fixing the navigation bar to become more simple and adjusting the size of the different types of content \that is displayed on the screen. If the developers chose to implement the same kind of style they did on the phone to the website that would be a major improvement. They have the format in their hands, just have not taken the time to implement online.
