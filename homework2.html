Homework Two

Prompt

You’re going to simultaneously learn some of the tools you might use for your first project AND critically analyze them as artifacts. Watch WeVideo’s Getting Started with WeVideo and vidIQ’s How to Post Your First YouTube Video. Your web site post this week will be a critical comparison of the two. Explain how images, text, callouts, transitions, editing, and narration were used in each of the two videos to teach the subject matter. Examine how the narrator or “speaker” is presented and how they “interact” with the viewer. Which video did you find more helpful and why? How did you use the features of the video to assist in your learning (e.g. pausing, skipping, rewinding, etc.)? What did you learn about how to design your own instructional video project by reviewing these?

Response

In the How to Post your First Youtube Video, the narrator has a much different approach in the previous video discussed. He is infant of the camera for most of the video which makes it very personable to the viewer. The editing is also very different in the sense that it is moves very quickly. For example, the quick zoom ins on where he is clicking or the several arrows pointing at different things he is discussing. This is beneficial to the viewer, because it allows them to understand what is being talked about and why it is helpful to them. The editing is fast paced which allows for a very easy to watch. Additionally, the referencing to the other videos he has discussing other topics is very helpful to the viewers who are watching for specific reason.

Each video is very helpful for different reasons and also for different people. Getting Started with WeVideo is slower and takes the time to explain topics in detail, while Ho to Post your First Youtube Video is straight to the point and answers the viewers questions as fast as they can. Personally, Getting Started with WeVideo is the type of tutorial I find to be more understanding and educational. The editing makes the video more organized and easy to comprehend.

The feature that YouTube provides is pause, play, rewind, closed captions, and even a playback speed. This tools allow for a seamless learning experience for whoever is watching to adjust these settings to fit them best. Playing, pausing, and rewinding allows the user to practice the things being shown at the same time and practice with the instructor. Closed Captions allow for people to read along when listening depending on how they learn best. Playback speed allows for other users to listen to the entire video but faster or slower depending on what they want to listen to more closely or less closely. The tools provided by Youtube specifically is a great way for viewers to have the please of choosing the type of experience they want to have.

The videos provided are very beneficial, because it included two things I will have to do for this project. I have never edited a video or posted a YouTube video and this gave me a preview of what I will be needing to do. WeVideo is a great tool for editing, and might be what I use to edit the video. YouTube is where the final product will be posted and watching the 5 minute video on how to makes it very easy for me to know how to when I complete the project.
