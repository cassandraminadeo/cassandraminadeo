Homework Four

Prompt

Discuss your views on the use of AI-assisted writing tools like ChatGPT, Google Bard, Bing AI, ZenoChat, etc. You should consider the benefits, challenges, ethical considerations, and the role of human creativity in the writing process. Connect with a chat bot of your choice (we used https://chat.openai.com/ in class) and ask it what it thinks the benefits and drawbacks of its use are. Try to challenge it on at least one of those ideas and see how it responds. In your prompt, reflect on your argument with the bot and consider how “human” the bot seemed to be and why this is important (or unimportant) to human-computer interaction. Experiment with the conversation to see if you can get it to seem more human to you and discuss what kinds of prompts you tried using and to what effect.

Response

AI is not a new concept, but has really only become popular and available to consumers relatively recently, with this, comes many challenges for teachers, student, employers, and employees. AI-associated tools, like everything has its pros and cons. Personally, ChatGPT has been incredibly helpful when learning new ideas, methods, and tools with coding. AI generates a better explanation, due to the fact that is basically a glorified Google. ChatGPT functions on pattern recognition and searching the web to answer the inputted question with information that matches. This makes needing a quick answer unbelievably efficient. On the downside, it is not always quite correct in understanding what I am specifically asking for. Not everything on the internet is correct and while AI tools can try its best to filter the incorrect sources out, it is not a perfect system.

In this moment in time, everything on the internet is an ethical dilemma. Every tool that we are provided is what the user makes of it whether that is going to be ethically positive or ethically negative. In regards to education and open source data, using AI generated tool like ChatGPT will and has caused issues of putting out work that the author has not written themselves. Right now, the process to ensure that text is original and accurate is something that is still in the editing process to avoid plagiarism.

Creativity in the writing process is not an easy thing to replicate, especially when tools like ChatGPT specifically take information that is already on the internet. This would result in repetitive results instead of originality. Beneficially, wiring AI tools can be helpful in the non creative aspects of writing, but is not something that is realistic when writing text such as fiction novels.

What are your pros and cons?

As an AI language model, I don't have personal experiences or feelings, but I can outline some general pros and cons of AI language models like myself:

Pros Cons

In ChatGPT’s immediate response, it writes, “I don’t have personal experiences or feelings.” The times when users interact with AI generated chats the niceties are directly implemented into the code so the writing feels less robotic. ChatGPT or any other tool needs direction from the user which relates to the human-computer interaction that we will not be able to remove. Any everyday user when trying to find a simple answer may not need (or want) those novelties and polite introductions to the wanted information, but for other people they may find it less awkward when they are using a “chat” feature. We don’t have this expectation for our search engines, and having it for AI generating tools also feels a little unimportant.

It is a challenge to make the generator itself more human, but it is programmed to have niceties when chatting with the user. Although it claimed multiple times that it has no emotions it is coded to have a mock sense of sympathy, kindness and understand. For example, when bringing up that I am sad ChatGPT apologizes and says I can share more if comfortable. While not directly having opinions it is coming off more human by showing kindness and sympathy for my mock sadness.
